Closing the Behavioral Intelligence Gap in K–12 Education

How StudentScope's real-time behavioral data platform unifies teachers, parents, and students — and why it represents a category-defining acquisition opportunity in EdTech.

The Problem

Behavior tracking is inefficient in schools. Teachers are forced to use infantilizing sticker-charts in order to monitor behavior even at the mid-high level tiers of public education.

Communication between teachers and their aides (who spend a majority of their time with students) is often challenging, difficult, and discouraging. Messages get lost, aides and paraprofessionals struggle to maintain consistent messaging, and ultimately, it is the student who suffers.

While studies are done and maintained throughout the scientific community including the current in-vogue use of the "ABC-principal" application of these processes is often lost to the chaotic nature of special education.

Ed Tech

Ed tech projects as a 32 billion dollar industry by 2030, and most of the tools that receive user-interface are excessively wasteful, or gamify every aspect of learning.

The current crop of incoming students have been raised with technology in every facet of their lives, and while they lack a basic understanding of how tech actually works, they have learned multiple workarounds for the systems they are given.

The encouragement of using games for education, while a noble pursuit for the augmentation of the educational journey, often forgets the actual goal of the process in the first place. The actual education of the user.

A modern student was raised with games that require them to fill in a series of blanks or answer a series of questions as rapidly as possible, with the reward being a sudden burst of dopamine-boosting pixels.

The goal in the mind of many students, suddenly does not become learning at all, it becomes filling in the blanks as mindlessly and as rapidly as possible in order to see the pixels that make happy-chemicals.

These games that are presented as educational certainly have their merit for classroom engagement, but at some point we have to question when the games go too far.

Companies like Gimkit and Blooket– like any tech company– require user engagement. The primary users of these companies are children. In order to grow the user base, the companies, in pursuit of profit, must appeal to children more and more.

Let's Use Ed Tech to Help the Actual Educators

StudentScope is a platform with the goal of streamlining the complicated gauntlet of communication that is student behavior tracking. It is designed by me, an educator and paraprofessional while working in an actual school. Student behavior (especially in middle-high grades) can be volatile and escalate rapidly. While peer-reviewed systems speak consistently of anticipating disruptive behavior in the classroom, the truth of the matter is that teachers and their aides are often over-taxed and simply are not given the mental bandwith to handle the over-abundance of students on a single caseload.

Behavior issues begin at the classroom level. In SPED this means that a behavior often must be relayed up a ladder that has an already rickety structure. The flow begins usually with an aide who notices a student misbehaving, occasionally, if the behavior is a consistent problem, the behavior will be eventually reported to the SPED teacher/case manager who may or may not make a note of it. After repeated instances of disruptive classroom behavior, disciplinary measures are slowly meted out, and finally a call is made home to a parent. This is an arduous process, and rarely is reporting consistent. In fact, the only consistent way the good lord and science have provided to the underpaid and overworked paraprofessionals is a behavior chart. In a world where every student is carrying a device to every class, and every teacher is issued a device for managing and maintaining professional communication, we are still using gold-sticker charts to track a student's behavior in the classroom is a disservice to the community that works endlessly to improve a students already challenging educational experience.

This seems a bit crazy, but it makes some sense when one considers the nature of public education. The first issue is the aide. Most teachers aides are extremely dedicated to their jobs and their students, in spite of the fact that their wages never reflect this. These lovely people, driven by their passion for their special-needs students are often older, and intimidated by technology. A sticker chart has it's benefits. It's bullet-proof. It's simple. There is simply not an app that can match that simplicity (Until now). Aides also are not spending their entire day with a single student. To avoid burnout with problematic cases, Aides trade the students from their caseload throughout the day. The hectic nature of the school day often does not allow the aides to communicate with one another to figure out what is going on with an individual student. It happens all the time. If a student has lost their device privileges because of their behavior in one class. When they are in another class, another aide usually has no idea about the consequences of their behavior in a previous period. If aides are struggling to communicate with one-another effectively, imagine how little of this information makes it to the case manager, let alone administration. These behaviors, as a result, have little impact or benefit to a student, and cannot be applied to an IEP or 405 (the very document designed to augment a struggling student's education.). With all of this information flying around unchecked and unsupervised, combined with the need of a case manager to sort, file and compile data from various and oft-lost paper charts, a ton of classroom behavior is going under-reported, and data points are being under utilized. In short, invaluable field research is scattered to the wind.

Recent behavior trends among current middle schoolers has led to the creation of StudentScope. It's a behavior-tracking app to facilitate communication between aides, teachers, administrators, and parents. It's a fourfold ecosystem with a bulletproof behavior-tracking app that acts just like a paper sticker chart. When I say "acts like" I mean it is as bulletproof and simple as an old-fashioned sticker-and-smilies chart but it provides modern data analytics, heatmapping, and instant cross-platform communication in real time. Any classroom aide or teacher can use the app to log the behavior in just a few words. Teachers have a dashboard that tracks these behavior logs, and allows them to communicate instantly with parents. AI assistance from Anthropic's Claude aids in professional language, and weekly/daily logs can be saved/printed.

Educators are saved approximately 40 hours a month on IEP prep and documentation, not to mention the constant labor spillage used for face-to-face discussion of classroom misbehavior.

40 hours per month * 500k SPED teachers is a net savings = 20 million hours/year. This does not account for school counselors, substitute teachers, aides, and others who can also use this app.
